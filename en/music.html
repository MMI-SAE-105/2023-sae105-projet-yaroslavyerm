Elegance in Sound: Lana Del Rey's 'Young and Beautiful' in 'The Great Gatsby'

by Yaroslav Yermakov published Saturday, October 21 at 10:18

In the realm of cinematic masterpieces, Baz Luhrmann's 2013 adaptation of F. Scott Fitzgerald's iconic novel, "The Great Gatsby", stands out not only for its stunning visuals and storytelling but also for its captivating soundtrack. Among the musical gems that are used in this film, Lana Del Rey's "Young and Beautiful" is a beautiful song that weaves seamlessly into the narrative, enhancing the overall experience.

Capturing the Essence of the American Dream

Released as a single in April 2013, "Young and Beautiful" conveys Gatsby's love for Daisy Buchanan. Lana Del Rey's ethereal vocals, accompanied by a lush orchestral arrangement, create a melancholic atmosphere that mirrors the complexities of the characters' relationships. The song's lyrics resonate with the central theme of the film, capturing the fleeting nature of youth, beauty, and the American Dream.

Lyrically Reflecting Characters' Complexities

The song's opening lines, "I've seen the world, done it all, had my cake now", mirror the opulent and hedonistic lifestyle of Jay Gatsby, a self-made millionaire who throws extravagant parties in a futile attempt to win back his lost love. Del Rey's vocals exude a sense of longing and vulnerability, echoing Gatsby's devotion to a past that can never be reclaimed.

The Ongoing Question of Love and Time

The chorus, with its poignant repetition of "Will you still love me when I'm no longer young and beautiful?" encapsulates Daisy's inability to commit to Gatsby, even though he still loves her unconditionally. This line resonates as a reflection of the film's central question: can love transcend time, societal constraints, and personal flaws?

An Orchestral Elegance

The orchestral arrangement, featuring sweeping strings and piano melodies, adds a layer of sophistication and grandeur to the song. It evokes the extravagant lifestyle of the Roaring Twenties while underscoring the underlying sadness that permeates the characters' lives.

A Musical Masterpiece that Lingers

"Young and Beautiful" is not just a soundtrack; it is a musical embodiment of the film's themes and emotions. Lana Del Rey's mysterious vocals and lyrics, combined with the orchestral arrangement, make it an essential element of the movie's overall ambiance. It is a song that lingers in the viewer's mind long after the credits roll, much like the enigmatic allure of "The Great Gatsby" itself.

"Young and Beautiful" is a masterpiece that perfectly complements the cinematic brilliance of "The Great Gatsby." Lana Del Rey's evocative performance and the song's lyrical depth make it an integral part of the film's enduring charm.
